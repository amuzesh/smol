There are many breathtaking views of these colorful rocks from trails into the canyon and from overlooks on the rim of the canyon.

At the bottom of the canyon, you'll see a thin, blue ribbon of water. This ribbon is the Colorado River.

The Colorado created the Grand Canyon. It probably took about 6 million years for the Colorado River to carve the canyon. The Colorado is still at work. The Colorado has dug valleys and carved beautiful ridges, hills, and other rock formations. It has help from ice and other forces.
