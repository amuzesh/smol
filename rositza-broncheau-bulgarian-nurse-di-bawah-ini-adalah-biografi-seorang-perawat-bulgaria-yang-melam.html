Jawaban dari "Rositza Broncheau – Bulgarian nurse Di bawah ini adalah biografi seorang perawat Bulgaria yang melam..."

Jika ente sedang melihat jawaban atas soal Rositza Broncheau – Bulgarian nurse

Di bawah ini adalah biografi seorang perawat Bulgaria yang melamar pekerjaan di United Kingdom. Gunakan informasi dalam biografi untuk menjawab pertanyaan setelahnya.

When she was a child, Rosita wanted to be a doctor not a nurse born on 1st July 1973 in Plovdiv. Her first memories are of playing “doctor “ – her favourite game. First She use her dolls as patients and later she used her friends while her father lounging in front of the TV set, Rosita would bandage his arm and legs and pop a thermometer into his mouth.
After primary school, there followed four years at 27 secondary school Vazov. Rosita worked hard but she didn’t like academic work and so did not do well enough to became a doctor. However, she scored an average of 5 in the diploma of completed secondary education and this was enough for her to get into the Medical University in Sofia in 1992 to take a BSc degree in nursing.
While she was studying, she fell in love with a medical student called Ilya botched. They had a short romance and married in 1994. In some ways it was a mistake, a year later, Ilya left to work in Syria, fell in love with someone else while he was there and on January 15th May 1996 Ilya and Rosita was discovered. Rosita was pregnant where Ilya left for Africa and on 14th 1995, she had a son, Ivan. Rosita kept her married name.
Rosita’s first job was at the Pirogue Emergency hospital in Sofia. She stays for two years, getting as much experience as possible and also extending her qualifications with a Certificate in Orthopaedic Nursing. She also studied English in her spare time and in October 1997 she passed the Cambridge FCE. She left the Pirogue in November 1997 and took up a post as staff nurse at the Aleksandrovsk Hospital. She also moved into a new flat at number, Gurov street Sofia 1602.
All this time, Ivan was growing up. It was difficult for Rosita to be a mother and have a career. Money was tight and, when she heard that UK NHS trusts were recruiting nurses from outside the UK, she decided to apply for a job.
Her sisters was already in England and, besides, Rosita wanted to leave behind unhappy memories, and make a fresh start. She decided that if she got a job there, she would go alone, settle and sent for Ivan later.
